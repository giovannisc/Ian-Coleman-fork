# Proposal: Theoretical Concepts Tutorial

## Purpose
Teach foundational Bitcoin concepts before users interact with derivation settings.

## Target Audience
- Beginners who understand basic finance but are new to self-custody.
- Intermediate users who need conceptual clarity before advanced operations.

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand why Bitcoin custody differs from traditional account custody.
- Understand mnemonic vs seed vs private key at a conceptual level.
- Understand trust minimization and self-sovereign responsibility.

## Suggested Module Outline
1. Why Bitcoin custody is different.
2. Recovery model: mnemonic and irreversible loss risk.
3. Threat model basics (online, physical, social engineering).
4. Wallet standards as interoperability contracts (high-level BIPs).

## Suggested In-App Activities
- Concept checks before unlocking tool actions.
- “What could go wrong?” scenario cards.
- Glossary with contextual examples.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Users can explain custody responsibilities in their own words.
- Users can differentiate mnemonic/seed/private key correctly.
- Users complete short checkpoint questions with high confidence.

## Open Questions for Discussion
- Mandatory vs optional concept checkpoints.
- Depth level for non-technical audience.
- Whether this tutorial must be completed before tool usage.
